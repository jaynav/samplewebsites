buying a bicycle

well buying a bicycle is more than just going to your local supercenter and getting the cheapest bike.

the one thing that one must consider when buying a bicycle is how a person is going to use a bicycle

  1. is one going to use the bike at recreational bike, commuter bike, or a everyday shopping bike(one that can hold your groceries)
  2. if recreational how is one going to use that bike
    1. on the mountains
    2. on the street
  3. if commuter
    1. does one need a bike for convenience
    2. does one need a for comfort
  4. if you are using it for small errands in your local community
    1. will i need a tricycle for carrying groceries
    2. will i need an adapter so i can take my kids along with me as well

if one has came to the conclusion of what one needs a bicycle for, it is always a good thing to ask oneself a very important question.

will i be using this bicycle a lot?
am i too lazy to pedal by myself?

the reason that this question is very important to ask is because one should always consider how much one is willing to spend on a bicycle.

bicycle cost ranges from about $49 dollars for a bike at walmart and target to bicyles costing well over  $7000 dollars at a bike shop.

although a majority of of the people would never go over $1000 dollars for a bicycle, one still has to decide how much one is going to spend. if one is only going to use the bicycle once a year, then i would suggest that you buy your bike at wal-mart. there is no point to spending lots of money from going to a bike shop if you are rarely going to use the bicycle. however, you will be treated better than staff at target.

if you do decide that that you are too lazy to pedal yourself, well expect to end up paying more than $600 dollars for an electric bike. the benefits of an electric bike is that untill the battery runs out, you will not have to pedal.
