choosing the right commuter bike

if you decided that a commuter bike is right for you, you have some options to choose from:

  1. road bikes that are made for comfort
  2. foldable bikes

Road bikes

well there are two types of road bikes, one of them is used for competition  and then there are the other ones that are made for complete comfort. Majority of these road bikes can be custom made to suit your needs. normally these bikes are custom made, shop owners measure the person buying the bicycle in order to get the custom bike to the measurements that will allow the user to have maximum comfort.

some of these bikes in the forks, have dampers built in the bike in order to absorb the shock of any bump in the road. however they look exactly like a competition road bike with the difference that the handle bars are much bigger and have a much wider seat then regular conventional road bikes

the cost of these bikes tend to range from $78 to about $2000 dollars. however a good comfortable road bike cost on the average of about $300-$400 at a bike shop or and $78- $300 at Wal-Mart/target

foldable bikes

well what exactly are fodable bikes? well foldable bikes are exactly that. they are foldable road bikes that are meant to be folded up and be taken inside of buses as well as trains. these bikes however are not as comfortable as one would expect. but if you plan to use public transit with your commute these bikes are very convenient with your travels since the fold up to a size of a fully loaded backpack.

however these bikes are not the cheapest bikes. these bikes have a selling range of  $329 to about $3000 dollars. so you can not go to target and buy a cheap $100 dollar bike. but the cost is worth it if you use public transit since if the bike bays are full, you will not have to wait for the next train or bus to show up.
